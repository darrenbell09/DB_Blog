My Approach

Infrastructure is often treated as a cost center—a necessary evil. I see it differently. Done right, infrastructure is a strategic capability that enables business velocity, reduces risk, and creates sustainable competitive advantage.

My philosophy centers on three principles: simplicity over cleverness (the system you understand at 3am during an outage is the system that matters), automation as leverage (toil compounds just like technical debt), and teams over technology (no architecture outlives the people responsible for it).

Areas of Expertise

Cloud Architecture

I design and implement multi-cloud architectures across AWS, Azure, and GCP with a focus on reliability, security, and cost efficiency. My experience spans landing zone design, network architecture, workload migration, and building the guardrails that allow engineering teams to move fast without breaking production.

Infrastructure Operations

As a Director of Infrastructure Operations, I've built and led SRE and operations teams responsible for platforms supporting thousands of engineers and mission-critical workloads. I apply SRE principles pragmatically—SLOs, error budgets, and blameless post-mortems as tools for trust, not bureaucracy.

Technology Leadership

I've grown engineering teams from individual contributors to senior engineers and managers. I care deeply about technical culture: psychological safety, engineering excellence, and creating environments where people do the best work of their careers.

Why This Blog

The infrastructure and cloud leadership space has no shortage of vendor content and conference talks polished to a shine. What it lacks is practitioners sharing what actually happened—the messy realities of legacy migrations, the organizational battles behind a platform team charter, the day an SLO conversation changed how a whole organization thought about reliability.

That's what I write. If you're a cloud architect, infrastructure leader, or senior engineer navigating similar terrain, I hope you find something useful here.
